The July Revolution

The July Revolution, popularly known as "36 July," was a historic uprising in Bangladesh that began in mid-July 2024 following the Supreme Court's decision to reinstate a 30% quota in government jobs for the descendants of freedom fighters.

This verdict triggered massive outrage among students and job seekers who demanded a fair, merit-based recruitment system, leading to the formation of the Anti-Discrimination Student Movement. On July 15, peaceful protesters at Dhaka University were attacked by groups aligned with the ruling party's student wing, marking the beginning of violent clashes.

Between July 16 and August 5, the country witnessed brutal crackdowns by police, ruling party activists, and government forces, leaving over a thousand people dead, more than twenty thousand injured, and thousands arrested. This period of bloodshed became known as the "July Massacre."

The Legacy of 36 July

The July Revolution, or "36 July," became not just a protest against job quota policies but a nationwide movement that toppled a long-standing authoritarian regime and reignited hopes for democracy, justice, and a new future for Bangladesh.
